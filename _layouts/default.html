About Kristin & Stephanie

Stephanie Coleman (fiddle/banjo) and Kristin Andreassen (guitar/clogging) developed their duo sound around campfire jams in West Virginia and dusky Brooklyn watering holes (such as the Lowlands Bar where they’ve hosted a Monday night session for the past five years). They play rural stringband tunes with a deep respect for the tradition, but like their favorite old timers, they also keep the door open to modern influences. If you think you’ve heard them offer up a Lucius-ish unison lead vocal or a stringband cover of Taylor Swift's “Welcome to New York”, your ears are not mistaken.

Kristin (of Uncle Earl and the song "Crayola Doesn't Make a Color for Your Eyes") is a songwriter, percussive dancer and in-demand square dance caller who merges those skills in performances and recordings that are both "inventive" (The New Yorker) and deeply rooted in American traditional music. 2015 saw the release of her second solo album Gondolier, with tours supporting Black Prairie, Dawn Landes and Aoife O’Donovan. Her debut record Kiss Me Hello included the award-winning “Crayola” song which has been covered by dozens of artists ranging from Tyne Daly to high school marching bands. kristinandreassen.com

Stephanie is a widely-respected fiddle and banjo player whose dad brought her to the renowned Appalachian String Band Music Festival in Clifftop, WV every summer since she can remember. She’s a perennial finalist in the contest there, and has recorded and toured with luminaries of that scene including banjoist Adam Hurt, Cleek Schrey, and The Red Squirrel Chasers. She studied audio documentary production at Duke University and put her radio skills to use when she produced a series on Bill Monroe's centennial for NPR News, NPR Music, and American Public Media. audiomydarling.com

This fall, Stephanie and Kristin will be taking their duo tour to Nova Scotia, where Kristin once studied square dancing and Stephanie has never been.
